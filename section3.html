Section 3: Commitments That Lead to Christ

Inviting action, and walking with people as they follow through

The Problem
The Disconnected Invitation

A lesson ends, and almost as an afterthought: "Will you read this chapter and pray about it?" The investigator agrees — but the invitation felt bolted on, unrelated to what was just taught. And often, no one follows up, so the commitment quietly fades. The principle was taught, but it didn't lead anywhere.
"Faith, to be faith, must center around something that is not known. Faith, to be faith, must go beyond that for which there is confirming evidence." Boyd K. Packer, in "The Candle of the Lord"

A commitment is the bridge between hearing truth and acting in faith. When it flows naturally from the principle just taught, it isn't an add-on — it's the next logical step on the path.

Core Skill
The Commitment Grows From the Principle

"Faith is not to have a perfect knowledge of things; therefore if ye have faith ye hope for things which are not seen, which are true." Alma 32:21

The pattern in Alma 32 is a seed: hear the word, then act on it — even with imperfect knowledge — and watch it grow. Every commitment you extend should be a "seed" planted directly from the principle just taught, something small enough to act on, but real enough to produce fruit.

Core Skill
Plan the Follow-Up Before You Leave

"Wherefore, settle this in your hearts, that ye will do the things which I shall teach, and command you." 3 Nephi 18:14 (paraphrased context)

A commitment without a planned follow-up is easy to forget — for the investigator and for you. Before ending the lesson, agree on exactly when and how you'll check in. This isn't pressure; it's care. It tells the investigator their progress matters to you.

Role-Play Scenario
Extending and Responding to Hesitation

Missionary: We've talked today about how repentance means we get to start again, with Christ's help. This week, is there something specific you'd feel comfortable starting to work on, even something small?

Investigator: I don't know... I've tried to change things before and it never really works.

Missionary: That makes sense — a lot of us have felt that way. The difference here is you wouldn't be doing it alone. What if, this week, you just prayed each night and asked Heavenly Father to help you with one small thing — even if you're not sure it'll work yet?

Investigator: Okay... I could try that.

Missionary: That's great. Would it be alright if we called you Thursday evening, just to see how it went?

"And now, my brethren, I wish from the inmost part of my heart, yea, with great anxiety even unto pain, that ye would hearken unto my words." Alma 13:27

Notice: the missionary didn't dismiss the hesitation or push past it. They acknowledged it, lowered the bar to something achievable, and locked in a specific follow-up time.

Practice
Respond to Hesitation

An investigator says: "I want to come to church, but I'm worried people will judge me because of my past." Write a response that acknowledges their concern, gently restates the invitation, and moves toward a specific plan.

Takeaway
Before You Extend a Commitment

"Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him." Revelation 3:20

Ask yourself: does this commitment grow directly out of what we just taught? Is it something they can realistically do? And have we agreed on when we'll talk about how it went? A commitment isn't the end of the lesson — it's the doorway to the next one.
