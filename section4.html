Section 4: The Heart of Each Lesson

Distilling the core message of every principle we teach

Bringing It Together
One Question for Every Lesson

Now that you've practiced staying focused (Section 1), asking simple questions (Section 2), and extending meaningful commitments (Section 3), this section ties it all together. For each of the four major lesson areas, ask yourself: "What is the ONE thing an investigator must walk away understanding?" Everything else — scriptures, questions, commitments — should support that one thing.
"And thou shalt declare glad tidings, yea, publish it upon the mountains, and upon every high place, and among every people that thou shalt be permitted to see." D&C 19:29

Four Lessons, Four Core Messages

Tap each lesson area to reveal its core message. Read it, then try writing your own version in your own words below it before checking the model answer.

Restoration ▸
"And also all they who receive this priesthood receive me, saith the Lord; for he that receiveth my servants receiveth me." D&C 84:35-36 (context)

Core message: God's true Church and authority, lost from the earth, have been restored through the Prophet Joseph Smith — so the fulness of the gospel is available again today.

See a sample one-sentence summary
"God speaks to us again today, the same way He always has, through a living prophet."
Plan of Salvation ▸
"For behold, this life is the time for men to prepare to meet God; yea, behold the day of this life is the day for men to perform their labors." Alma 34:32

Core message: God has a plan, centered on Jesus Christ, that explains where we came from, why we're here, and where we can go — and that plan makes it possible for families to be together forever.

See a sample one-sentence summary
"Life isn't random — God has a plan for you, and it's built around being with the people you love, forever."
Gospel of Jesus Christ ▸
"Wherefore, the right way is to believe in Christ, and deny him not; and Christ is the Holy One of Israel... and have faith in him... repenting of your sins, and witness unto the Father that ye are willing to take upon you the name of Christ, by baptism." 2 Nephi 25:16, 29 (paraphrased)

Core message: Through faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, baptism, receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end, we can be forgiven, become more like Christ, and return to live with God.

See a sample one-sentence summary
"Jesus Christ gives us a path — through faith, change, and covenants — to become who God wants us to be."
General Missionary Lessons (Overview) ▸
"Verily, verily, I say unto you, he that receiveth whomsoever I send is he that receiveth me; and he that receiveth me receiveth my Father; and he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom." D&C 84:36-38 (context)

Core message across all lessons: Every lesson, in its own way, is an invitation to come closer to Jesus Christ — and through Him, to feel God's love and find lasting peace.

See a sample one-sentence summary
"No matter which lesson we're teaching, we're really always inviting someone to feel closer to God."

Teaching With Clarity and Love

"And now, behold, I say unto you, this is the order which I have given unto my servant Joseph... bring forth and establish the cause of Zion." D&C 21:1, 6 (paraphrased context)

You now have a simple framework: stay focused on one principle, ask simple questions and listen, extend commitments that flow naturally, and always know the one thing you want someone to understand. This isn't about teaching less — it's about teaching what matters most, in a way people can actually receive. As you apply this, watch how investigators respond with more openness, and how your own confidence grows.
